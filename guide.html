Currency Converter

Available on the Home page

The currency converter is the core feature of RateFlow. It converts any amount between 170+ currencies using live mid-market rates — the same rates banks use when trading with each other. Unlike banks, we show you the real rate, not a padded version.

How to use it

  1. 1Type the amount you want to convert in the top input field. You can enter any number — whole or decimal.
  2. 2Click the left dropdown (From) to choose your starting currency. Use the search box inside the dropdown to quickly find any currency by name or code (e.g. type "XAF" or "franc").
  3. 3Click the right dropdown (To) to choose your target currency. The same search works here.
  4. 4The result appears automatically as you type — no button needed. The result uses thousand separators (e.g. 303,500.25) so large amounts are easy to read.
  5. 5Use the ⇅ swap button to instantly reverse the conversion — useful when you want to see both directions quickly.
  6. 6Click Copy Result to copy the full conversion to your clipboard (e.g. "500 USD = 303,500.00 XAF") for pasting into messages or documents.
  7. 7Click the star icon to save this pair to your Favorites for instant access next time.

Tip: The rate shown (e.g. "1 USD = 607.34 XAF") is the mid-market rate — the fairest rate available. If a bank quotes you less, they are charging a hidden margin. Use RateFlow's Bank Markup Finder to see exactly how much.

Live Rates Table

Available on the Home page

The Live Rates table shows real-time exchange rates for the most important currency pairs — all compared against USD. It's designed for quick reference: you can see at a glance what the dollar is worth in dozens of currencies without typing anything.

How to use it

  1. 1Scroll to the Live Exchange Rates section on the Home page.
  2. 2Use the filter buttons — All, African, or Major — to focus on the currencies most relevant to you. The "African" filter shows all supported African currencies in one view.
  3. 3Click or tap any row to jump to the converter with that currency pair pre-selected and ready to use.

Why it matters: Knowing the live USD/XAF or USD/NGN rate before you send money ensures you can spot when a bank or transfer service is charging a bad rate.

Rate Alerts

Available at rateflowcurrency.netlify.app/alerts

Rate Alerts let you set a target exchange rate and get notified when the market reaches it. This is powerful for anyone who regularly sends money abroad — instead of checking rates daily, you set your target once and let RateFlow watch for you.

How to use it

  1. 1Go to the Rate Alerts page.
  2. 2Choose your From and To currency. The live rate is shown below the selectors.
  3. 3Enter your target rate — the rate at which you'd want to act. For example, if USD/XAF is currently 607 and you'd send money when it hits 615, enter 615.
  4. 4Choose Above (alert when rate goes up) or Below (alert when rate drops).
  5. 5Click Set Alert. Your alert is saved locally in your browser — no account needed.
  6. 6Each time you visit RateFlow, the site checks your alerts against the live rate and shows a notification if your target has been reached.

Important: Alerts are stored in your browser (localStorage). They will work as long as you use the same browser and haven't cleared your site data. Alerts are checked each time you load a RateFlow page.

Favorites

Available at rateflowcurrency.netlify.app/favorites

Favorites lets you save currency pairs you convert regularly — like USD/XAF, EUR/NGN, or GBP/KES — so you never have to search for them again. Each saved pair shows the live rate and links directly to the converter.

How to add favorites

  • On the Home page converter: after selecting a pair, click the star icon button next to "Copy Result".
  • On the Favorites page directly: use the dropdowns to choose a pair and click Add.

How to use favorites

  1. 1Go to the Favorites page. All saved pairs are shown as cards with the live rate.
  2. 2Click Convert Now on any card to open the converter with that pair pre-loaded.
  3. 3Click the remove icon on any card to delete a saved pair.

Historical Rate Chart

Available on the Tools page

The Historical Rate Chart shows how an exchange rate has moved over the past 7, 30, or 90 days. Understanding rate history helps you decide when to convert — not just how much.

How to use it

  1. 1Go to the Tools page.
  2. 2Select your From and To currencies using the searchable dropdowns.
  3. 3Choose your time range: 7D (last 7 days), 30D (last month), or 90D (last 3 months).
  4. 4Click Load Chart. The chart displays with a line graph showing how the rate moved over the selected period.

How to read the chart

  • The Y-axis shows the exchange rate value (e.g. 607.34 for USD/XAF).
  • The X-axis shows dates. The rightmost point is the most recent rate.
  • An upward trend means your "From" currency is gaining strength — a good time to convert if you're selling that currency.
  • A downward trend means your "From" currency is weakening — you may want to wait if you expect a recovery, or act now if you think it will continue falling.

Note: For major currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, etc.), history is sourced from the European Central Bank via the Frankfurter API. For less common pairs, an estimated trend based on the current rate is shown.

Calculator Tools

Available on the Tools page

RateFlow's Tools page includes four calculators designed to answer the practical questions people face when dealing with foreign currency.

1 — Travel Budget Calculator

Enter your daily budget in your home currency, your destination currency, and how many days you'll be travelling. The calculator tells you your daily budget and total trip cost in local currency at today's live rate — essential for planning before you go.

2 — Transfer Fee Comparator

Enter the amount you want to send and your currency pair. The tool shows you how much your recipient would get via a typical bank (3% markup), a good transfer service (0.5%), and an airport kiosk (~8%). Seeing the difference in black and white is the fastest way to understand why choosing the right service matters.

3 — Rate Change Calculator

Enter the rate you've been quoted by a bank or service. The tool compares it to the current live mid-market rate and tells you the percentage difference — so you can see at a glance whether you're getting a fair deal.

4 — Bank Markup Finder

Similar to the rate change calculator, but focused specifically on identifying how much a bank's hidden markup costs you per 1,000 units sent. Useful for understanding the real cost of a quoted rate.

Tip: Use the Transfer Fee Comparator before every international transfer. Even a quick check showing you that a transfer service would deliver $40 more to your recipient than your bank is worth 30 seconds of your time.

Dark / Light Mode

Available on every page

RateFlow supports both dark mode (default) and light mode. Choose whichever is more comfortable for your eyes and environment.

  • Click the moon/sun icon in the top-right corner of the navigation bar to toggle between modes.
  • Your preference is saved automatically in your browser and remembered every time you return to RateFlow.
  • The setting persists across all pages — converter, tools, alerts, blog, and guides all follow your chosen theme.

Using RateFlow on Mobile

Optimised for all screen sizes

RateFlow is fully responsive and works on all mobile devices and screen sizes. Here are a few tips for the best mobile experience:

  • Tap the ☰ menu icon (top right) to open the full navigation menu. All pages are accessible from there.
  • The currency dropdowns include a search box — tap the dropdown and start typing the currency name or code to find it instantly without scrolling.
  • Use the inputmode="decimal" keyboard (shown automatically) for entering amounts on mobile — it shows numbers and a decimal point without switching to a full keyboard.
  • The rates table switches to a two-column layout on smaller screens. Tap any row to convert instantly.
  • Add RateFlow to your Home Screen for app-like quick access: in Chrome, tap the three-dot menu → "Add to Home Screen". In Safari, tap the Share icon → "Add to Home Screen".
