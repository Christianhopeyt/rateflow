But is it always the better choice? Here's the honest answer.

Fee Comparison

Let's use a concrete example: sending €1,000 from France to a US bank account.

  • Wise: ~€4.00 fee, recipient gets $1,073 (at mid-market EUR/USD 1.08)
  • Major French bank (SWIFT): €20 sending fee + 2.5% rate markup → recipient gets ~$1,027
  • Difference: $46 more with Wise on a €1,000 transfer

For transfers to Africa the difference is even larger. Banks often apply 3–5% markups on less-traded currency pairs like EUR/XAF or USD/NGN, while Wise's fee stays in the 0.5–1.5% range.

Exchange Rates

This is the most important factor most people overlook. The mid-market rate is the "real" exchange rate — the midpoint between global buy and sell prices. You can check it anytime on RateFlow.

Wise always converts at or within a fraction of a percent of this rate. Banks typically apply a 2–4% spread, meaning for every $1,000 you send, $20–$40 disappears quietly into the bank's margin — never shown as a fee line.

Transfer Speed

Wise has dramatically improved transfer speeds in recent years. Most transfers between major currencies (USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, CAD) arrive within hours or by the next business day. Some corridors are even faster due to Wise's local banking infrastructure — instead of routing through SWIFT, they move money between local bank accounts in each country.

Bank SWIFT transfers remain slower: typically 1–3 business days for developed market corridors, and 3–5 days for African or emerging market destinations, with occasional delays due to compliance checks or correspondent bank routing.

Limits & Eligibility

Wise supports individuals and businesses. Personal account limits vary by country but are generally $1M+ per transfer for verified accounts. You'll need to verify your identity (passport or national ID) for amounts above a certain threshold.

Banks have no practical upper limit for wire transfers, and some high-net-worth individuals prefer banks for very large transfers (over $500,000) simply for the established relationship and legal paper trail.

Security & Regulation

Wise is regulated by the UK's Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), the US FinCEN, and equivalent bodies in every market they operate. Customer funds are safeguarded in separate accounts — they are not lent out or invested. Wise has processed over $100 billion in transfers with a strong security track record.

Banks are regulated by central banking authorities and deposits are typically government-insured (FDIC in the US, FSCS in the UK, etc.). For pure regulatory safety, banks have the longer track record.

When to Use Each

Use Wise when:

  • You want the best exchange rate with low, transparent fees
  • You're sending to a supported country (check wise.com for coverage)
  • Speed of 1–2 days is acceptable
  • You're sending under $50,000

Use your bank when:

  • The destination country isn't supported by Wise or other services
  • You need to send a very large amount ($500,000+)
  • You need the institutional paper trail for legal or tax purposes
  • You're already inside a branch and it's a genuine emergency
