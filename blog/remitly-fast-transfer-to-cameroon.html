Cameroon is one of Central Africa's most connected economies, and sending money there has never been easier — or more confusing, given the number of services available. This guide focuses on what actually matters: what your recipient gets, how fast they get it, and what the transfer costs you.

Delivery Options in Cameroon

  • MTN Mobile Money (MoMo): The most popular mobile wallet in Cameroon. Available in urban and rural areas. Recipient needs an MTN Cameroon SIM with MoMo activated.
  • Orange Money: Orange's mobile wallet — widely used, especially in Francophone regions. Requires an Orange Cameroon SIM.
  • Bank account: All major Cameroonian banks accept international SWIFT transfers. Slower (1–4 days) but no phone number required.
  • Cash pickup: Western Union and MoneyGram agents are available in Douala, Yaoundé, and other cities. No account needed — just an ID.

MTN MoMo vs Orange Money: Which Is Better?

Both are excellent for receiving international transfers. The choice usually depends on which network your recipient already uses for their daily transactions:

  • MTN MoMo has slightly wider coverage in rural Cameroon and higher transaction limits for receiving international transfers.
  • Orange Money is preferred in some Francophone urban areas and has a strong merchant payment network.
  • Both charge no fee to receive money — only to withdraw cash from an agent or ATM (typically 1–2% of the amount).
