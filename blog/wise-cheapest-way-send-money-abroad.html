The Hidden Cost Problem

Sending $500 to family in Cameroon sounds simple. But depending on how you do it, your recipient might get the equivalent of $460 — or $490. That $30 difference is the hidden cost of the exchange rate markup, and most people have no idea it exists.

Banks rarely charge zero fees on international transfers. They charge two ways: a visible wire fee ($15–$50) and an invisible markup on the exchange rate (1–5%). The rate markup is where most of the money disappears, because it's embedded in the rate rather than shown as a fee.

Modern transfer services like Wise operate differently. They convert at or very near the mid-market rate — the same rate you see on RateFlow or Google — and charge a small, transparent fee upfront. The result: more money arrives at the destination.

Wise — Best Overall Value

Wise (formerly TransferWise) is the gold standard for transparent, low-cost international transfers. Their key advantage is simple: they always use the mid-market rate — the same rate you see on Google or RateFlow — and add a small percentage fee that's clearly shown before you confirm.

  • Fees: Typically 0.4–1.5% depending on currency pair and payment method
  • Exchange rate: True mid-market — no markup
  • Speed: Usually 1–2 business days; some corridors same-day
  • Coverage: 70+ countries; some African corridors supported
  • Best for: Transfers to Europe, UK, USA, Australia, some African countries

Limitation: Wise doesn't support direct XAF delivery to all countries. For Cameroon, check current availability — coverage is expanding. For EUR-denominated transfers to the CFA zone, it works well.

Remitly — Best for African Corridors

Remitly specialises in transfers to developing markets and has excellent coverage across Africa, including support for MTN Mobile Money and Orange Money in Cameroon.

  • Fees: From $0 for promotional first transfers; typically $3–$5
  • Exchange rate: Small markup (~0.5–1%) over mid-market
  • Speed: "Express" delivery in minutes; "Economy" in 3–5 days at better rates
  • Best for: Sending to Cameroon, Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, Senegal, and 50+ more African countries

Western Union — Best for Cash Pickup

Western Union is the right choice when your recipient needs cash in hand and doesn't have a bank account or mobile money wallet. With 500,000+ agent locations worldwide, they have unmatched physical reach in rural areas.

  • Fees: $5–$15 for most corridors when sending online
  • Exchange rate: ~1–2.5% markup, varies by corridor
  • Speed: Cash pickup available in minutes
  • Best for: Recipients without bank accounts; rural areas; emergency cash

Banks — When to Use Them

Banks are rarely the cheapest option, but they make sense in two scenarios: very large transfers (over $50,000, where specialist services may have limits) or when you need the paper trail and regulatory oversight of a major institution for business or legal purposes.

For everyday personal transfers — supporting family, paying rent abroad, receiving freelance income — a specialist service will almost always save you money.

Final Verdict

For most people sending money to Africa: Remitly for speed and coverage, Wise for the best rate on supported corridors. Always check the mid-market rate on RateFlow first so you know what "fair" looks like — then compare what each service actually delivers to your recipient.
