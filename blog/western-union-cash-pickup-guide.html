How Cash Pickup Works

Cash pickup is simple in concept: the sender initiates a transfer online or at a Western Union agent, and the recipient walks into any participating agent location, presents identification, provides the transfer reference number (MTCN), and receives cash on the spot. No bank account, no mobile wallet, no smartphone required.

What the Sender Does: Step-by-Step

  1. Go to westernunion.com or the WU app (or visit a physical agent location).
  2. Enter the destination country and amount. The fee and exchange rate will be shown clearly before you confirm.
  3. Select "Cash Pickup" as the delivery method.
  4. Enter the recipient's full name — exactly as it appears on their government ID.
  5. Pay with debit card, credit card, bank transfer, or cash (at agent).
  6. Save the MTCN (Money Transfer Control Number) — a 10-digit code you share with the recipient. This is their key to collecting the money.

What the Recipient Does

  1. Visit any Western Union agent location in their city or town. Use the agent locator at westernunion.com/find-locations to find the nearest agent.
  2. Bring a government-issued photo ID (national ID card, passport, or driver's licence).
  3. Provide the MTCN from the sender.
  4. Provide their full name (must match exactly what the sender entered).
  5. Receive cash in local currency.

The entire pickup process at the agent typically takes under 5 minutes.

Finding Agents in Africa

Western Union has strong agent networks across Africa. In Cameroon, agents are found at Express Exchange, Ecobank, UBA, and various postal offices in Yaoundé, Douala, Bafoussam, and other cities. In Nigeria, agents are available at banks including GTBank, First Bank, and Zenith Bank. Use westernunion.com/find-locations and filter by "Cash Pickup" and your destination country.
