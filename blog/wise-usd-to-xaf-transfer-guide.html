Sending money from the United States to Cameroon, Gabon, the Democratic Republic of Congo, or other Central African nations involves the XAF (Central African CFA Franc). Because XAF is pegged to the euro at a fixed rate of 655.957 XAF per euro, understanding the USD/EUR rate is the key to knowing what your dollars are really worth when they arrive.

Understanding the USD/XAF Rate

There is no direct floating USD/XAF rate. Instead, the conversion happens in two steps: USD → EUR (floating, changes daily) and EUR → XAF (always exactly 655.957). So the effective USD/XAF rate today is:

USD/XAF = (1 ÷ EUR/USD rate) × 655.957

If EUR/USD is 1.08, then 1 USD = (1 ÷ 1.08) × 655.957 = ~607.37 XAF. You can always check the live rate on RateFlow's converter.

Does Wise Support XAF?

Wise's support for XAF varies by corridor and changes over time. As of early 2025, Wise supports EUR-denominated transfers to bank accounts in Cameroon and several other CFA zone countries. The practical approach is to send in EUR — since EUR/XAF is fixed, your recipient's bank converts at the official rate automatically, meaning no markup on that final step.

Always verify current availability at wise.com before initiating a transfer, as coverage evolves.

Step-by-Step Transfer Guide

  1. Check the live USD/XAF rate on RateFlow so you know the fair baseline before you start.
  2. Create a free Wise account at wise.com. Verify your identity with a passport or national ID.
  3. Start a new transfer. Select USD as your sending currency. Choose EUR as the receiving currency (or XAF if available for your corridor).
  4. Enter the amount you want to send. Wise will show you the exact fee and the exact amount your recipient will receive before you confirm.
  5. Enter recipient details. You'll need their full name, IBAN or local bank account number, and bank name. For Cameroon, most major banks (Ecobank, Afriland First Bank, UBA, BGFI) accept SWIFT/IBAN transfers.
  6. Fund the transfer from your US bank account (ACH, typically free), debit card, or wire transfer.
  7. Track the transfer in real time via the Wise app or website.

Alternatives If Wise Doesn't Cover Your Corridor

  • Remitly — Excellent XAF coverage, supports MTN Mobile Money and Orange Money directly in Cameroon. Often the fastest option.
  • WorldRemit — Good African coverage, competitive rates, supports mobile money.
  • Western Union — Available in most Cameroonian cities for cash pickup; higher fees but maximum reach.
  • Wave — Growing in West Africa; limited Central African coverage as of 2025.

Tips for the Cameroonian Diaspora

  • Use mobile money where possible. MTN MoMo and Orange Money have very high penetration in Cameroon. Remitly delivers directly to these wallets, avoiding bank delays.
  • Send mid-week. Tuesday–Thursday typically offers slightly better rates due to higher forex market liquidity.
  • Avoid sending on weekends — many services widen their spreads when currency markets are closed.
  • Set a rate alert on RateFlow if USD/EUR is moving in your favour — even a 1% improvement on the EUR/USD rate means 6.56 XAF more per dollar sent.
