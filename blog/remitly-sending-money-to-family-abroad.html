For millions of Africans living in Europe, North America, and Asia, sending money home is not a financial transaction — it's a family obligation. Whether it's school fees in Cameroon, medical bills in Nigeria, or daily living expenses in Côte d'Ivoire, remittances from the diaspora are a lifeline. But too much of that money disappears into fees. This guide helps you keep more of it.

The True Cost of Remittances

The global average cost of sending $200 is around 6.2% according to World Bank data. For African corridors, it's often higher. That means for every $200 you send to your family, over $12 is going to financial institutions — not your family. At the Sustainable Development Goal target of 3%, that same transfer should cost no more than $6.

The good news: modern services like Remitly and Wise already achieve 1–2% fees on many African corridors. The gap between what most diaspora members currently pay and what they could pay is significant.

Building a Smart Transfer Strategy

Rather than sending whenever there's a need and using whichever service is most convenient, a small amount of planning makes a big difference:

  • Know your baseline rate. Check RateFlow before every transfer. A 1% swing in EUR/USD changes what your family receives by 6.56 XAF per euro sent.
  • Set up rate alerts. Use RateFlow's rate alerts to get notified when the rate moves in your favour.
  • Save your preferred service. Add your most-used currency pair to RateFlow Favorites so you always have the live rate at a glance.
  • Plan around big expenses. School fees, rent, and medical costs are often predictable. Transfer in advance when the rate is good rather than urgently when the rate may be poor.

Frequency: One Big Transfer vs Many Small Ones

A common question: should you send one large monthly transfer or smaller amounts more frequently?

  • Larger, less frequent transfers reduce the total number of fees you pay. If each transfer costs a flat $3–$5, fewer transfers means fewer fees.
  • Smaller, more frequent transfers allow you to average out exchange rate fluctuations (cost averaging) and give your family more predictable cash flow.
  • Best approach: A monthly scheduled transfer for regular expenses (rent, food) plus ad-hoc transfers for emergencies. Remitly allows you to schedule recurring transfers, which also sometimes qualifies for promotional rates.

Keeping Transfers Safe

  • Only use FCA, FinCEN, or equivalent regulated services. Remitly, Wise, WorldRemit, and Western Union are all properly licensed.
  • Never use unofficial "informal" transfer networks (called "hawala" in some regions) — while sometimes cheaper, they offer no legal protection.
  • Verify recipient mobile numbers carefully before sending to mobile money. Once delivered to a mobile wallet, transfers are generally irreversible.
  • Enable two-factor authentication on your transfer app accounts.
