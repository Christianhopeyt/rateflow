Cookie Notice

Last updated: January 2025

🍪 Short version: RateFlow itself does not place any tracking cookies on your device. However, our advertising partner Google AdSense and our analytics tool Google Analytics may do so. This page explains what those cookies are, why they exist, and how to control them.

1. Does RateFlow Use Cookies?

No. RateFlow does not set any cookies of its own. We store your preferences (theme, favorites, rate alerts, cookie consent choice) in your browser's localStorage — a different technology from cookies that never leaves your device and is never sent to any server.

What we store in localStorage:

  • rf-theme — your dark/light mode preference
  • rf-favorites — your saved currency pairs
  • rf-alerts — your rate alert settings
  • rf-cookie-consent — your cookie consent choice (accepted / declined)
  • erate_base_* — cached exchange rate data (expires after 1 hour)

None of this data is transmitted to RateFlow's servers. It stays on your device until you clear your browser's site data.

2. Who Does Use Cookies on Our Site?

Two third-party services that we embed on RateFlow may place cookies on your device:

2a. Google AdSense (Advertising)

We display advertisements on RateFlow through Google AdSense. Google, as a third-party advertising vendor, may use cookies to:

  • Serve you ads based on your interests and prior browsing activity across the web
  • Measure ad performance (how many people saw an ad, clicked on it, etc.)
  • Limit how often the same ad is shown to you
  • Prevent fraudulent ad clicks

These are Google's cookies — not ours. We have no control over what Google collects through them or how long it retains that data. Google's advertising is subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

Typical Google AdSense cookies:

2b. Google Analytics (Traffic Analysis)

We use Google Analytics (GA4) to understand aggregate traffic on RateFlow — which pages are most visited, what countries visitors come from, and what devices they use. This helps us improve the site.

Google Analytics may set cookies to distinguish users and sessions:

We have configured GA4 to anonymise IP addresses. We do not use GA4 data to identify individual users.

3. Your Choices & Opt-Out Options

  • Decline on the cookie banner: When you first visit RateFlow, a popup appears. Clicking "Decline" records your preference and prevents personalised ad cookies from being the basis of our consent. Note: Google may still serve non-personalised ads.
  • Opt out of Google personalised ads: Visit Google's Ad Settings or aboutads.info.
  • Opt out of Google Analytics: Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Clear all data: Go to your browser Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data → Cookies and site data. This removes all third-party cookies and resets your RateFlow localStorage preferences.
  • Browser cookie controls: All major browsers allow you to block third-party cookies entirely. This prevents Google AdSense and Analytics cookies but may affect other websites you visit.
