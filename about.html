Our Mission

Exchange rate information should be free, accurate, and accessible to everyone — whether you're a student sending money home to Cameroon, a freelancer invoicing a client in the US, or a traveller trying to figure out if that market stall in Tokyo is giving you a fair deal.

RateFlow was built on a simple belief: you deserve to know the real exchange rate before you convert a single cent. Not the bank's rate. Not the airport's rate. The actual rate.

Our Data Source

All exchange rates on RateFlow are sourced from open.er-api.com, which provides live rates for 170+ currencies updated every hour. For historical chart data, we use the Frankfurter API, which distributes European Central Bank reference rates.

Unlike the old Frankfurter-only setup, our current data source includes all African currencies: XAF, XOF, NGN, KES, GHS, ZAR, EGP, and 30+ more — making RateFlow genuinely useful for the African diaspora.

How We Stay Free

RateFlow is supported by two transparent revenue streams. First, we display non-intrusive advertising through Google AdSense. Second, some of the "Send Money" links on the site are affiliate links — when you sign up for a transfer service through RateFlow, we may earn a small commission at no cost to you.

We only recommend services we believe offer genuine value: transparent fees, fair exchange rates, and solid customer protection. Our editorial content is always written independently.

Privacy First

RateFlow does not collect personal data. No account required, no conversion history stored on our servers, no data sold to third parties. Currency rates are cached locally in your browser for performance — stored on your device, never ours.
